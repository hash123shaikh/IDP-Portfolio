## Purpose of Individual Development Plans (IDPs)

The IDP process at QIRAIL is designed to:

1. **Empower Personal Ownership**: Take responsibility for your career progression beyond your time at QIRAIL
2. **Ensure Mutual Benefit**: Make your time spent in the lab beneficial for both your growth and research goals
3. **Facilitate Communication**: Enable clear discussion of evolving goals with advisors
4. **Guide Skill Development**: Identify and develop targeted skills for current projects and future career

## Expected Outcomes

### For You (The Mentee)
- Clear understanding of short-term, medium-term, and long-term career goals
- Identified skill gaps and concrete plans to address them
- Structured approach to professional development
- Regular feedback and guidance from experienced mentors
- Documentation of progress and achievements

### For Mentors
- Understanding of your aspirations and career trajectory
- Ability to provide targeted support and opportunities
- Facilitation of your growth beyond current research projects
- Contribution to your long-term career success

## Key IDP Components

### 1. Career Goals Assessment
- Current strengths and skills
- Knowledge or experience gaps
- Long-term career aspirations (10 years)
- Medium-term objectives (post-graduation)
- Short-term goals (next year)

### 2. Research Skills Development
- Description of current research projects
- Skills already acquired
- Skills needed for project completion
- Training or course requirements

### 3. Career Skills Development
- Communication skills (writing, presentations)
- Teaching and public outreach
- Mentoring capabilities
- Leadership and management development

### 4. Progress Planning
- Planned presentations (oral/poster)
- Anticipated publications
- Funding applications

### 5. Career Transition Preparation
- Professional networking contacts
- Reference letter sources
- CV and research summary development
- Professional development activities

## Communication Guidelines

### Frequency of Interactions
- **Individual Meetings**: Minimum 30 minutes before Feb 15, 2026
- **Quarterly Reviews**: Progress discussions
- **Annual Committee Meeting**: Comprehensive review
- **Ad-hoc**: As needed for project guidance

### How to Prepare for Meetings
1. Review and update your IDP before each meeting
2. Prepare specific questions or discussion points
3. Bring documentation of recent achievements
4. Reflect on challenges and seek guidance
5. Come with proposed solutions, not just problems

### Effective Communication Tips
- Be honest about challenges and setbacks
- Celebrate achievements, big and small
- Ask for help early when needed
- Provide updates proactively
- Be open to constructive feedback
